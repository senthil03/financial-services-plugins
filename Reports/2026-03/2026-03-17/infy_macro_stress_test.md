## 2. Valuation Stress Test (The "$13 Floor")

Let's look at the math required to break $13:

*   **Current EPS Est**: ~$0.73 (INR 73.1)
*   **Current Price**: ~$13.26
*   **Current PE**: **~18.1x**

### Scenario A: The "Technical Dip" ($12.50–$13.00)
*   **Implied PE**: **~17.1x**
*   **Probability**: **Medium (Short-term)**
*   **Driver**: Sentiment-driven "wash-out" or currency fluctuations. Historically, this has been an "Auto-Buy" zone for institutional investors.

### Scenario B: The "Global Crisis" (<$12.00)
*   **Implied PE**: **~16.4x**
*   **Probability**: **Low**
*   **Requirement**: A global recession worse than 2008 or a total structural failure of the Indian IT business model. INFY has not traded at these PE levels for a sustained period in over a decade (the 10-year mean is ~22x).

---

## 3. Why $13 is Likely the Hard Floor

1.  **The INR Support**: Major Indian support clusters are at **₹1,085–₹1,150**. At current exchange rates, ₹1,085 translates exactly to ~**$13.00**. There is massive institutional buying power sitting at this level.
2.  **Cash Conversion**: Infosys has a free cash flow conversion of **113%**. It is a "Cash Cow." Even if the market panics, the dividend yield and buyback potential become too attractive to ignore below $13.
3.  **The IT Paradox**: When the world economy is "bad," companies focus on **cost-saving**. Infosys’s core competency is taking over expensive IT operations and running them cheaper. Bad macro conditions often increase their deal pipeline (e.g., the $4.8B Q3 win).

---

## Final Verdict: Is it "Too Bad"?

**No.** The global economy is not "bad" in the traditional sense; it is **transitional**. We are moving from "Cheap Money" to "AI-Efficiency." 

While the *stock price* is suffering from technical weakness (hitting 52-week lows), the *underlying economy* and *corporate IT budgets* are still growing.

**Suggested Strategy**: 
Don't fear the macro headlines. If INFY touches $12.90–$13.10 due to a temporary market panic, it is statistically more likely to be a **generational buying opportunity** than the start of a collapse.
