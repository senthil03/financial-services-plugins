## 🏗️ The Infrastructure Paradox: $37.5B CapEx vs. $141 Oil
Microsoft is currently in the most aggressive capital cycle in its history. Q2 FY26 CapEx hit **$37.5 Billion** (66% YoY increase), driven by the build-out of custom silicon AI clusters and data center clusters in Japan and Singapore.

### The Problem: Powering the Cloud
With Brent crude at **$141/bbl** (Spot), the cost of electricity—essential for cooling and operating these massive AI hubs—represents a significant structural headwind:
- We expect a **110–130 bps impact on Cloud Gross Margin** if oil remains above $120/bbl for the duration of Q3 and Q4.
- Azure’s 39% growth is impressive, but "profitable growth" requires an immediate pivot to energy-neutral data center clusters.

### The Solution: AI Efficiency Premium
MSFT’s **Azure MAIA 100** and **Cobalt 100** silicon are now in general availability. These chips offer a **40% better performance-per-watt** compared to generic GPU clusters. In a high-energy-cost world, Microsoft's vertical integration becomes its strongest defensive moat.
